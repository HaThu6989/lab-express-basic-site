Pablo Picasso's Works

What Was Picasso’s Best Work?

Picasso’s “Guernica”, in addition to being one of his most famous works, is also one of the most searched paintings in the world on Google. As a visual prelude to the coming atrocities of World War II, it depicts an aerial bombing raid over the Basque town of Guernica in April 1937 and the war that followed.

Did Picasso Have Other Jobs?

In fact, Pablo Picasso played an important role in the development of 20th-century art as a pioneering artist who experimented and innovated for over 92 years. In addition to being a master painter, he was also a sculptor, printmaker, ceramicist, etching artist, poet, and author.

Was Picasso Successful Before He Died?

Picasso was extraordinarily wealthy when he passed away at the age of 91 – thousands of paintings he had created, five properties, a large savings account, gold and bonds among other things. Many famous artists died in abject poverty during their lifetimes. There was no will in his estate, however.

How Did Picasso Become Successful?

It was Picasso’s responsibility to support others that brought him success. In keeping integrity of his work while also maintaining financial stability, he hoped he would act as a role model for determination and creative risk-taking.
